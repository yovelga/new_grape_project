
\chapter{Conclusions}
\label{chap:conclusions}

\section{Summary of Contributions}
\label{sec:summary_contributions}
\noindent
This study successfully demonstrated that visible--near-infrared (VIS-NIR) spectral imaging can reliably detect table grape cracking ('Scarlotta' cultivar) under uncontrolled field conditions, even during the early stages prior to severe macroscopic degradation. The physical mechanism enabling this detection is deeply rooted in the biomechanics of the crack: the destruction of the waxy cuticle and subsequent cellular water loss produce a highly distinct, depressed reflectance signature, particularly in the NIR plateau and the red-edge inflection.

\medskip\noindent
Transitioning from controlled laboratory pixel-level analysis to whole-image field deployment exposed significant challenges related to background clutter, illumination variance, and extreme class imbalance. These challenges were systematically mitigated through a novel pipeline that combined a multi-class supervised learning approach with geometric spatial aggregation (patch-density blob filtering).

\medskip\noindent
This spatial logic proved that while spectral data is necessary for identifying the chemical and structural changes of a crack, spatial context is mandatory for suppressing the geometric noise inherent to the agricultural environment. 

\section{Revisiting the Research Hypotheses}
\label{sec:revisiting_hypotheses}
\noindent
The empirical results of this study directly address and validate the core research hypotheses established in the introductory chapter:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Hypothesis 1 (Spectral Distinguishability):} Confirmed. The study confirmed significant physical differences in the spectral signatures of healthy and cracked tissues. 
    
    \item \textbf{Hypothesis 2 (Pixel-Level Classification):} Confirmed. The supervised XGBoost architecture effectively learned these spectral decision boundaries, achieving near-perfect classification performance (PR--AUC $\approx 1.0$) on isolated pixels, definitively proving algorithmic feasibility.
    
    \item \textbf{Hypothesis 3 (Whole-Image Robustness):} Confirmed. While initial binary models struggled with background clutter, the transition to a 10-class background model coupled with spatial aggregation successfully isolated cracks from highly deceptive elements (e.g., dry wood), proving that robust full-image detection is achievable in the field.
    
    \item \textbf{Hypothesis 4 (Early Detection Capability):} Confirmed. The spatial-spectral pipeline successfully identified early-stage micro-cracks before they became fully visible macroscopically, achieving an independent test F1\textsubscript{CRACK} of 0.800 under early-stage conditions.
    
    \item \textbf{Hypothesis 5 (Spectral Efficiency):} Confirmed. Through Backward Feature Selection (BFS), the original 159-band spectrum was successfully distilled into an optimal subset of 30 wavelengths without compromising accuracy. This proves that massive spectral redundancy exists and that full-range hyperspectral data is not strictly required for operational deployment.
\end{itemize}

\section{Future Research Directions}
\label{sec:future_work}
\noindent
Future research should focus on several primary avenues to bridge the gap between this proof-of-concept and commercial agricultural deployment.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{First,} cross-cultivar validation is essential; the spectral markers identified for ``Scarlotta Seedless'' must be tested against grape varieties with differing cuticle thicknesses and anthocyanin profiles.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Second,} the development of a hybrid preprocessing method that retains absolute albedo information alongside SNV-normalized spectral shapes could significantly reduce residual false positives caused by bright background artifacts.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Third,} the hardware implementation of the selected 30-wavelength configuration into a physical, real-time multispectral camera must be validated under dynamic vineyard driving conditions.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Fourth,} future work should examine whether grape-cluster spectra change systematically under different irrigation regimes. Because cracking is linked to rapid water uptake, such shifts---especially in the NIR water-absorption region---may help identify early signs of cracking susceptibility before visible symptoms appear.
    
\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Finally,} further algorithmic refinement of the whole-image pipeline should explore stronger spectral--spatial integration strategies for reducing the remaining structural false positives under field conditions.
